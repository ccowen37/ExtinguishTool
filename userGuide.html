The data for the database has multiple sources. In the smoker identification process, our team primarily used the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) dataset and the Public Housing Developments dataset from the U.S. Department of Urban Housing and Development (HUD). Additional datasets we used to assist with our analysis include HUD Income Limits, and State Cigarette Excise Tax Rate.

The following datasets were used for mapping:

  • Public Housing Developments:
  • Federally Qualified Health Centers
  • Medical Centers
  • Hospitals
  • Home Health Services
  • Indian Health Service Facilities
  • Veteran Affairs Facilities

Our Return on Investment (ROI) calculations considered a public housing development and state health perspective. Each perspective considered different interventions and costs when calculating the ROI. Our team researched multiple sources to gather the probability of success and costs of each intervention.
